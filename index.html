Research

I am interested in using information on both birds and parasites to ask evolutionary questions. Some of my current work is on toucan biogeography, screening for malarial parasites in the Philadelphia area, and an upcoming project on great horned owl phylogeography.

Teaching

I have taught a variety of classes as a graduate student including introductory biology labs and evolution discussion sections. As an undergraduate, I also was a teaching assistant for a field ornithology class and helped with a marine field methods class.

Public Outreach

I am committed to promoting interest in science and ornithology through public outreach events both at the museum and in the community. Some recent highlights include a girl scouts outreach day, science night at the Lawrence Beer Co., and ornithology collection tours.
